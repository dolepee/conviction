The receipt is the product.

Conviction re-reads Polygon instead of trusting a success message. Substituting the wallet, market token, amount, shares, exchange, order ID, or chain makes verification fail closed.

The paid service returns the pre-execution position card, not a claimed fill. The controlled proof dossier is a separate retrospective check of one house-confirmed execution; its reference intent expired before settlement and is not presented as the source of that fill.

Independent receipt proof

A valid fill returns its actual total debit, shares, average price, verification checks, and deterministic proof hash.

Hard boundary

Conviction bounds intent. Your wallet decides whether to execute.

No signals, no hidden sizing, no custody, no leverage. You choose the market, side, and economic bound; the website produces a card, while the official Polymarket plugin and your Agentic Wallet handle any signing.

New Polymarket deposit wallets use max token allowances and blanket outcome-token approvals to official exchange contracts. Conviction discloses that setup, recommends a dedicated low-balance wallet, and does not ask anyone to bypass an organization’s wallet policy.

Polymarket V2 applies venue fees at match time rather than inside the signed order. Conviction therefore derives a conservative fee reserve, requires the dedicated wallet balance to cap exposure, and verifies principal, fee, and total debit independently after settlement.
