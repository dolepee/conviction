The receipt is the product.

Conviction re-reads Polygon instead of trusting a success message. Substituting the wallet, market token, amount, shares, exchange, order ID, or chain makes verification fail closed.

A paid endpoint returns a signed pre-execution card, not a claimed fill. The buyer-side runner validates that card, handles the exact wallet action after fresh consent, and then verifies the result. On July 22, a controlled signed v4 intent settled inside its five-minute issuance window. Conviction independently verified the issuer signature, both CTF outcome tokens, the canonical Polygon block, and every fill bound. A separate controlled live gate completed payment to proof in 69 seconds. These are house acceptance proofs, not a cold external native-path run or traction.

Published controlled OPEN settlements recorded zero venue fee. Conviction enforces nonzero fee bounds in deterministic and adversarial tests, but does not claim a live nonzero-fee proof yet.

Hard boundary

Conviction bounds intent. Your wallet decides whether to execute.

No signals, no hidden sizing, no custody, no leverage. You choose the market, side, and economic bound. The buyer-side agent calls the services and official plugin itself; this website remains a free preview, manual card inspector, and proof verifier. Your Agentic Wallet handles every signature.

OPEN currently requires an already-ready buyer-controlled Polymarket deposit wallet. First-time venue setup is Polymarket-managed and may require two reusable pUSD allowances plus three reusable outcome-token operator approvals. Conviction will not bypass a wallet or organization policy, and it will not charge an unsupported buyer.

Polymarket V2 applies venue fees at match time rather than inside the signed order. Conviction therefore derives a current fee reserve, rechecks it immediately before execution, and verifies principal, fee, and total debit independently after settlement. The venue fee is not misrepresented as signed.
